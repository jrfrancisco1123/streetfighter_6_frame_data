The term 'frame' in fighting games is used to represent the smallest unit of time. One frame is measured 1/60 of a second. Every action in Street Fighter 6 for a character such as walking, jumping, blocking, and attacking can all be measured in frames. In Street Fighter 6, every move in a characters command list has certain attributes associated with them called frame data. This is traditionally broken down to different attributes

    Startup frames: The beginning portion of the move before it can make contact with your opponent
    Active frames: The portion where the move is active and can hit your opponent
    Recovery frames: The end portion of the move that can no longer make contact with your opponent. During this time you cannot perform any other actions

Frame data gives us the information about a characters move set whether if that move is fast or slow, if it's safe or unsafe against a blocking opponent, or what kind of effect it has when a move makes contact against your opponent. The properties for a move usually determines if it's more favorable than others as you may see during a match, your opponent can use that move frequently. It's not neccessary to memorize the data for all the characters but simply knowing the properties of a particular move if it has a fast startup for example will allow you to act before your opponent thus putting you in an advantagous position in battle
